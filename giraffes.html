GIRAFFES

Giraffes are the tallest land animals on Earth, native to the savannahs and open woodlands of sub-Saharan Africa. Adult giraffes can grow up to 5.5 meters (18 feet) tall, with their necks alone reaching about 2 meters (6 feet). They use this height advantage to reach leaves high in the trees, particularly from acacia species, which make up a large part of their diet. Each giraffe has a unique coat pattern, which also helps with camouflage and temperature regulation.

Giraffes live in loose social groups and communicate through body language and infrasonic sounds. Males sometimes engage in "necking," a behavior where they swing their necks to spar for dominance. Despite their size, giraffes are graceful and can run up to 60 km/h (37 mph). Their height and excellent eyesight also help them spot predators from far away. Giraffes typically give birth standing up, and their calves can walk within a few hours of birth.
